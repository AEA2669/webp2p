Your Data, Your Rules

Privacy & Security

FileDrop was built with one principle: your data should never leave your control. Here's how we keep it that way.

End-to-End Encrypted

All data is encrypted via WebRTC's built-in DTLS encryption. Only the two connected peers can read the data.

No Server Storage

Files and messages are never uploaded to any server. Data flows directly between devices through a peer-to-peer connection.

No Accounts Required

No sign-up, no login, no email. A random 4-digit code is all you need. It changes every time you reload.

Zero Tracking

No analytics, no cookies, no tracking pixels. We don't know who you are, what you share, or when you use it.

What is FileDrop?

FileDrop is a free, open-source tool for sharing files and collaborating between devices without any middleman. Built on WebRTC technology, it creates a direct encrypted tunnel between two browsers — no apps to install, no accounts to create, no data stored anywhere.

Whether you're sending a photo from your phone to your laptop, collaborating on notes with a colleague, or just need to quickly move some files — FileDrop makes it effortless and private.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked

Is there a file size limit?
There's no hard limit. Files are transferred in 64KB chunks via WebRTC. In practice, files up to several GB work well. Speed depends on your internet connection since data goes directly between peers.
Do both devices need to be on the same network?
No! FileDrop works across any network. WebRTC uses STUN/TURN servers to establish the initial connection, then data flows directly between peers regardless of their location.
What happens when I close the page?
Everything is gone. Nothing is saved on any server. Your connection code, chat history, notepad content, and transfer history only exist in your browser while the page is open. Refreshing gives you a new code.
Can someone intercept my files?
WebRTC connections use DTLS encryption by default, making it extremely difficult for anyone to intercept the data. The 4-digit code is only valid while both pages are open.
